Elastic Gridshell

Elastic gridshells are efficient and lightweight structures for freeform architecture.

A rationalized way to build freeforms

An elastic gridshell is a freeform structure, generally doubly curved, but formed out through the reversible deformation of a regular and initially flat structural grid

Building curved shapes through this process seems to offer the best of both worlds : shell structures are amongst the most performant mechanically speaking while planar and orthogonal constructions are much more efficient and economic to produce than curved ones.

This constructive system is highly standardized and requires only one type of profile and one type of connector... but it leads to an infinity of possible curved shapes.

Advanced nonlinear beam models

Because the grid undergoes large bending deformations to produce a volume, the computation of the structure is complex and requires non standard structural analysis models.

Torsion can severely affect the resistance of the profiles and the equilibrium geometry of the structure. Our beam model accurately takes into account axial, bending and torsion mechanical behaviors.

Our formfinding software relies on the latest research works in this field, providing accurate computation of the geometry of the grid.

Toolbox

Our tools are developed as standalone C# API libraries. They are seamlessly integrated to Rhino & Grasshopper design platform.

Compass (meshing)

This app gives full control over the meshing of a given 3D surface. The software produces a Chebyshev net that can be reconstructed flat on the ground. It can deal with holes.

Marsupilami (formfinding)

This app takes a flat Chebyshev net and a set of boundary conditions and computes the geometry of the deformed grid. Requires advanced nonlinear beam models.

Bridge (analysis)

This app is a bridge to conventional structural analysis softwares such as Oasys GSA. It autogenerates the model with all the 3D load cases (wind, snow).

Production

This app provides a set of scripts to produce manufacturing-ready informations for drawings or CNC machining.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Who invented elastic gridshells ?

    The concept was invented by Frei Otto, a German architect and structural engineer who devoted many years of research to gridshells. In 1975 he designed the Multihalle of Mannheim, a 7500m2 wooden shell which demonstrated the feasibility of this technology and made it famous to a wide audience. However, despite their potential, very few projects of this kind were built after this major realization. And for good reason, the resources committed at that time cannot guarantee the replicability of this experiment for more standard projects, especially on the economic level. Moreover, the technics and methods developed by Otto’s team in the 1960s have mostly fall into disuse or are based on disciplines that have considerably evolved. New materials, such as composite materials, have recently emerged. They go beyond the limitations of conventional materials such as timber and offer at all levels much better technical performances for this kind of application. Finally, it should be noted that the regulatory framework has also deeply changed, bringing a certain rigidity to the penetration of innovations in the building industry. Therefore, the design of gridshells arises in new terms for current architects and engineers and comes up against the inadequacy of existing tools and methods

  • What materials are most suitable to elastic gridshells ?

    Materials that have a good strength but also a good bendability. Traditional materials such as wood, steel or aluminium can do the job. But when it comes to bendability composite materials such as glass fibre reinforced polymers really make the difference and they are quite affordable nowdays.
